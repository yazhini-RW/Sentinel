How honeybees communicate and forage

When a worker bee finds a good source of nectar, it returns to the hive and performs a movement called the waggle dance. The direction and length of the dance tell the other bees which way to fly and how far to go to reach the flowers.

Honeybees are important pollinators. As they move from flower to flower collecting nectar and pollen, they transfer pollen between plants. Honeybees help pollinate a large share of the fruit, vegetable, and nut crops that people eat.

A foraging bee may visit hundreds of flowers on a single trip. Bees see ultraviolet patterns on flowers that are invisible to the human eye, which helps them find nectar quickly.
