Honeybee colony structure

A honeybee colony is made up of three kinds of bees: one queen, thousands of female worker bees, and a smaller number of male drones. During the summer a healthy colony contains roughly 20,000 to 60,000 worker bees.

The queen is the only bee that lays eggs. A productive queen can lay up to about 1,500 eggs per day. Worker bees are all female and cannot reproduce; they do almost all of the work in the hive. Drones are male, they have no stinger, and their main purpose is to mate with a queen.

A worker bee born in summer lives for only about six weeks, because it wears itself out foraging. Worker bees raised in autumn can live for several months and help the colony survive the winter.
